Was your take-home pay for your last Universal Credit period £935 or less?

Take-home pay is used to work out your Universal Credit payments.

If you don't work, your take-home pay will be £0.

Where to find your take-home pay

If you have a Universal Credit account online

  • View your online Statement.
  • Find the section under the heading 'Take-home pay'.
  • Your take-home pay will be found next to the text 'Your total take-home pay for this period is £x.xx'.

Do not enter the total taken off your take-home pay

If you get a monthly Universal Credit payment letter

Your take-home pay is shown on your Universal Credit monthly payment letter.

It's usually on page 2 under the heading 3. We then take account of your take-home pay.
