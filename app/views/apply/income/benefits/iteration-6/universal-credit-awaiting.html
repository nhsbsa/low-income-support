While you're waiting on your Universal Credit decision

Until your first assessment period is complete, Universal Credit does not entitle you to help with NHS costs.

If you need to pay for any NHS charges, for example, prescriptions or dental treatment, make sure you get a receipt and ask how to claim a refund.

If you pay for a prescription, ask for a refund receipt called an FP57 when you pay - you cannot get one later. Find out more about refunds for NHS prescription charges (opens in a new tab).
